How to Merge PDF Files

Combine PDFs Securely in Your Browser

Our PDF merger uses the powerful pdf-lib library to combine PDF documents entirely in your browser. Unlike cloud-based tools like iLovePDF or Smallpdf, your files are never uploaded to any server, giving you complete privacy and security for confidential documents.

Step-by-Step: How to Merge PDF Files

  1. Upload your PDFs by clicking the drop zone or dragging multiple PDF files onto the page.
  2. Reorder the files using the up/down arrow buttons to set the desired page order.
  3. Click "Merge & Download PDF" to combine all files into a single document.
  4. Your merged PDF downloads automatically, ready to share or print.

Common Use Cases for Merging PDFs

  • Students: Combine multiple assignment pages, notes, or research papers into one submission file.
  • Job Applicants: Merge your resume, cover letter, and certificates into a single application PDF.
  • Business Teams: Combine reports, invoices, or contract pages from different departments into one document.
  • Legal Professionals: Merge case documents, affidavits, and exhibits into a single court filing.

Why Choose Our PDF Merger Over Competitors?

Most online merge PDF tools upload your files to remote servers. That means your contracts, financial statements, and personal documents are sitting on someone else's computer. Our merger is different because everything happens inside your browser. We use the pdf-lib JavaScript library to copy pages directly from your source files into a new combined document, without re-encoding or quality loss.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is this PDF merger really free?

Yes, completely free with no limits on file count or size. No watermarks are added to your merged document.

Are my PDF files uploaded to a server?

No. Everything is processed in your browser. Your documents never leave your computer, making this the most private PDF merger available.

Can I reorder PDF files before merging?

Yes! Use the arrow buttons next to each file to move it up or down in the order. The final PDF will follow your custom arrangement.

Is there a limit on how many PDFs I can merge?

There's no hard limit. Performance depends on your device's memory. Most modern devices can handle dozens of PDFs smoothly.

Will the quality of my PDFs be affected?

No. We copy pages directly from source documents without re-encoding, so quality remains identical to the originals.

Does this work on mobile phones?

Yes. Our PDF merger works on all modern browsers including Chrome, Safari, Firefox, and Edge on both Android and iOS devices.
